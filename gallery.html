Traditional Calligraphy

Chinese calligraphy

Chinese calligraphy is one of the oldest and most refined forms of calligraphy in the world. It is characterized by its flowing lines and elegant brushstrokes. Chinese calligraphy is often used to express philosophical or religious ideas, and it is also seen as a form of art.

Japanese calligraphy

Japanese calligraphy is similar to Chinese calligraphy, but it has its own unique style. Japanese calligraphy is often used to write poetry or short stories, and it is also seen as a form of meditation.

Arabic calligraphy

Arabic calligraphy is characterized by its intricate patterns and geometric shapes. Arabic calligraphy is often used to write the Quran, and it is also seen as a form of art.

Indian calligraphy

Indian calligraphy is characterized by its bold lines and vibrant colors. Indian calligraphy is often used to write religious texts, and it is also seen as a form of art.

Tibetan calligraphy

Tibetan calligraphy is characterized by its flowing lines and simple designs. Tibetan calligraphy is often used to write Buddhist scriptures.

Peruvian calligraphy

Peruvian calligraphy is characterized by its use of geometric shapes and bold colors. It is often used in textiles and other decorative arts.

Contemporary Calligraphy

Abstract calligraphy

This type of calligraphy uses bold, gestural strokes to create abstract shapes and patterns. It often has a fluid, organic feel.

Geometric calligraphy

This type of calligraphy uses geometric shapes and patterns to create a sense of order and symmetry. It often has a clean, minimalist feel.

Calligraphic lettering

This type of calligraphy uses traditional calligraphy techniques to create lettering that is both beautiful and functional. It is often used for logos, signage, and other decorative purposes.

Mixed media calligraphy

This type of calligraphy incorporates other media, such as paint, ink, or collage, to create a more dynamic and visually interesting effect.

Bold, expressive lines

Contemporary calligraphers often use thick and thin lines to create a sense of movement and energy in their work.

Abstract shapes and forms

Contemporary calligraphers often break away from traditional forms and create more abstract shapes and forms with their letters.

Hand-lettering & Design

Calligraphy

This is a formal type of hand-lettering that is characterized by its flowing lines and elegant curves.

Brush lettering

Script lettering:

This type of hand-lettering imitates cursive handwriting.

Typography

This is the art of arranging type in a visually appealing way.

Lettering for logos

This type of hand-lettering is used to create logos for businesses and organizations.

Lettering for typography

This type of hand-lettering is used to create typographic designs, such as posters, flyers, and packaging.

Modern Calligraphy

Script

Script calligraphy is a style of calligraphy that imitates cursive handwriting. It is often used for invitations, logos, and other decorative purposes.

Brush

Brush calligraphy is a style of calligraphy that uses a brush pen to create thick and thin strokes. It is often used for Chinese and Japanese calligraphy.

Uncial

Uncial calligraphy is a style of calligraphy that is characterized by its rounded letters. It was popular in the Middle Ages and is now often used for decorative purposes.

Blackletter

Blackletter calligraphy is a style of calligraphy that is characterized by its thick, heavy strokes. It was popular in the Middle Ages and is now often used for decorative purposes.

Italic

Italic calligraphy is a style of calligraphy that is characterized by its slanted letters. It was developed in Italy in the 15th century and is now often used for writing.

Gregorian

Gregorian calligraphy is a formal, structured style that was developed in the Renaissance. It is characterized by its sharp angles and precise lines.

Letters and Fonts

Script fonts

Script fonts often imitate cursive handwriting, so they can suggest images of flowing curves and elegant flourishes. For example, the script font Brush Script can suggest images of flowers, ribbons, and other delicate objects.

Display fonts

Display fonts can be very decorative, so they can suggest images of bold shapes and bright colors. For example, the display font Impact can suggest images of fireworks, traffic signs, and other attention-grabbing objects.

Monospace fonts

Monospace fonts have a fixed width, so they can suggest images of columns of text, computer code, or other orderly arrangements. For example, the monospace font Courier New can suggest images of spreadsheets, typewriters, and other tools associated with data entry.

Uncial

This style of calligraphy is characterized by its rounded letters and flowing curves. It was originally used for writing on scrolls, but it is now also used for decorative purposes.

Gothic

This style of calligraphy is characterized by its angular letters and sharp points. It was originally used for writing on illuminated manuscripts, but it is now also used for posters and other decorative purposes.

Blackletter

This style of calligraphy is characterized by its thick, heavy strokes and dark letters. It was originally used for writing in legal documents, but it is now also used for decorative purposes.
